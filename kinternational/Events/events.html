  • KRITKALA

    MARCH 3, 2022 | KRITASH

    Kritash and Dramatizers collaborated to celebrate Raksha Bandhan, featuring a play about Raja Bali and Lord Vishnu's incarnation. Poetry, speeches, and Kritkala's ethics teachings enriched the event. It fostered community bonds, welcoming workers and their children to learn about our culture through entertainment, especially meaningful for those separated from their families.

  • .

  • Teaching The Needy

    MARCH 3, 2022 | KRITASH

    The Kritash club at IIT Jammu hosts "Eye On Social," a recurring series of brief videos offering insights into institution activities. Students use this platform to express their views and highlight event aspects they appreciate. These informative videos are now posted bi-weekly, enhancing student awareness and fostering a sense of community, participation, and feedback.

  • .

  • Donation Drive

    MARCH 3, 2022 | KRITASH

    Kritash, committed to aiding the underprivileged, conducts frequent donation drives where students are urged to contribute unused items that can make a difference in the lives of those in need. Donations encompass clothing, footwear, books, and stationery, with a focus on ensuring that every item is in good condition for gifting. These donations bring joy to the less fortunate and a sense of fulfillment to the donors.

  • .

  • Cleanliness Drive

    FEBRUARY 4, 2022 | RSHAH421

    Kritash led a transformative cleanliness initiative at IIT Jammu, uniting students, faculty, and staff to enhance the campus environment. The campaign included a thorough litter clean-up effort where enthusiastic volunteers used trash bags to revitalize neglected areas. This drive not only improved aesthetics but also promoted environmental stewardship, fostering a more responsible and cleaner community.

  • .

  • Health Checkup Camp

    JANUARY 12, 2022 | RSHAH421

    Kritash, in partnership with SIBA, organized a free multispecialty health checkup for the welfare of IITians on September 22, 2023. Amandeep Hospital, Green Court Hospital, and Sehora Dental College participated. The event was inaugurated by Prof. Vijayan Palippattu and Dr. Karunika. Students, faculty, staff, and residents received free checkups, boosting doctor's morale. Services included eye tests, dental care, and more. The event concluded with acknowledgments for those who contributed to its success.
